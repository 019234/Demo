What this is
A Playable Teaser

Demo is a gothic survival adventure game where players control a teacher faced the horrors of the invasion. Inspired by the style and atmosphere of The Midnight Walk and the tense survival gameplay of the Little nightmares games. players must endure the horror of the invasion (yes, war), think fast, As she journeys deeper into the nightmare, she must confront loss, fear, and the darkness that threathens to consume everything.

The demo's job is not to sell the game. It is to make the player understand what they are agreeing to before they start it.

Why Clara, not Kita

If the demo protagonist were Kita, the player would know she survives she's the main character. That safety would remove every threat in the demo. Clara has no such protection. The player builds a relationship with her in twenty minutes and the game ends it without asking permission. She will appear as an empty house in the main game's opening chapter. The player will recognize it. They will not need to be told what happened there.

Connection to Main Game

Clara is Kita's neighbor not a blood relation, a village teacher, the teacher every likes. Kita passes her empty house in the end of Act I. The empty house does not explain itself. The player already carries the explanation.

People in the demo
Two sisters, one building

The demo has two main characters. everybody in this demo is distinct enough to remember their name. if the players can remember everyone just from their voice or look, the weight of the story is much greator much more effective, where the death is in literal every corner, the question remains, what will happened to the main 2 characters.

Clara Reyes
Playable  ·  Age 28  ·  Schoolteacher
Not a fighter. A woman who taught children to read and cared whether they understood. She has never done anything wrong in her life just normal woman. loved her sister, showed up to work. The war did not ask her opinion before arriving at her school.
What the demo does to her, She arrives at the last room already changed not by choice but by passage. The crawl, the soldier, the blood on her hands that the hallway put there without asking. She opens the last door. She closes it. She walks to the window. That is all she has left.
Lena Reyes
NPC  ·  Age 14  ·  Clara's younger sister, student
She writes on her books with care and draws flowers in the margins of her books. She is the only reason Clara moves toward the fire instead of away from it. SHE is not a plot device she is the specific face that makes the crawl possible. Without Lena there is no reason to go deeper. The demo earns its ending because of her.
in the first minutes it shows the relationship between the sisters, the age gap is wide enough that others think the're mothers, but the relationship is much more closer to bestfriends teasing and genuine talks which makes the ending of the demo that much tragic

The Last Room
One door left intact. Clara has been moving toward this door since the artillery hit through the hallway, through the rubble, through the soldier. She opens it. Red. Only red. The walls. The floor. The small clothes. She does not scream. She stands in the doorway and the sounds disappear not faded, gone, as if the world muted itself. Her face holds nothing. Not grief. Not horror. She closes the door. She leaves
MECHANICS: None. The player cannot enter the room. Cannot look away. The door opens and closes and that is all the game allows.

The Distance  Demo End
The camera follows Clara out of the window and then slowly falls behind. She walks into the back, The camera does not follow. It turns back to the school the building burning in full, the entrance gone, the roof caving inward. The same orange light from Scene 2, returned but wrong now. The same color. the school slowly getting consumed by the fire, Cut to black. No continue prompt. Main menu loads.
MECHANICS: Full removal of player agency. The camera is the only perspective. The building is the last image not Clara, The school. What it was. What it is now. That is the demo's final word.

Combat
Knife
Introduced: Soldier grapples Clara
The game takes control away completely. Clara is pinned. The interact prompt appears while she is underneath him. The player presses it and holds it. No cutaway, no score, no sound cue on completion. It ends when he stops. The exit is right there after. The player chooses to stay.
The exit being visible is the mechanic. The choice to not use it is the game's only question about who Clara is.
Systems Design
Eight mechanics

this mechanics are still to think about, they're not concrete. but in the demo, the mechanics needs to be limited due to scope of the game

Movement
Crawling
Introduced: The hallway, after the fire
The ceiling has collapsed to crawling height. Standing is physically impossible at the threshold Clara crouches automatically when she reaches it. No button, no prompt. What changes is perspective, speed, and sound. The world is larger from the floor. She is smaller in it.
Physical fact before mechanic. The crawl means something because she could still stand, once.
Stealth
Shadow hugging
Introduced: Near a blown window, fire scene
The player tries to stand near the blown window. A soldier's head turns. Clara backs against the wall. The shadow is alive. Light is death. No explanation given the failed attempt teaches it. From here the player reads every room for its dark side first.
Discovered by failure. Never explained. The player teaches themselves.
Stealth
Distraction throw
Introduced: Blocked corridor, fire scene
Loose debris near a blocked passage. Clara can interact with it she kicks it or displaces it. A soldier turns toward the sound. One second of his back. That is the window. Introduced once, with clear cause and effect. After that the player scans every room for throwable objects.
Shown once clearly. Trusted after that.
Stealth
Breath holding
Introduced: Soldier passes within two feet
The game slows. A single prompt appears: hold the button. Clara holds her breath ambient sound drops, replaced by her heartbeat and his footsteps. He moves on. The prompt never appears again. The player presses it every time a soldier is close, without being asked, for the rest of the demo.
One prompt in the entire demo. After this, the player supplies the tension themselves.
Environment
Corpse drag
Introduced: Body blocking east corridor
A body is blocking the only path east. No prompt, no arrow. The player moves Clara close she crouches beside it. The interact button appears. She drags it clear. The act takes five seconds and costs something the game never measures. Introduced as a traversal problem. Lands as a moral weight.
The moment the player understands what they are dragging is the moment the mechanic changes category.

Level Design
One school, six spaces

The entire demo exists inside one school building and its immediate yard. This is not a scope compromise it is a deliberate choice that makes the horror intimate. There is no outside world to run to. The building that was safe becomes the building that is killing her, and the player never leaves it.

Asset Efficiency Note

The hallway is the same corridor traversed twice from different heights. The crawl sequence requires no new geometry same walls, same floor, different camera angle and smoke particle overlay. Classroom B requires a single interior visible only through a doorway. The yard is a single exterior establishing shot with a fire particle system. Every major emotional beat is achieved through camera angle, lighting, and sound design rather than new geometry.

The Final Frame

The camera turns back to the school while Clara walks toward the something. The building burns. The orange light from the sunset the same color, the same total warmth is back, and it is from the fire now. The demo ends on that image because it is the only honest ending

No continue prompt. No chapter select. The main menu loads. The player sits with it.

The demo's last job is to make the player hesitate before starting the main game. Not because they don't want to play. Because they understand, now, what playing means.
